34Arrondel, Calvo Pardo, Giannitsarou, and Haliassos (2020) provides an example of this approach. In a survey of French households, they not only elicited respondents’ stock market expectations, but also the size and ﬁnancial expertise of the social circles within which they discuss ﬁnancial matters. The paper ﬁnds that social interactions aﬀect stock market beliefs mostly through information channels, instead of social preferences.
